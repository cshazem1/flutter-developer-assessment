# Exercise 6: Video Walkthrough — Debug a Live App

| Detail     | Value          |
|------------|----------------|
| **Time**   | ~20 minutes    |
| **Points** | 20             |
| **Type**   | Screen recording |

---

## Why This Exercise Exists

This exercise tests your **real debugging skills** — not what you can Google or ask AI.
We want to see how you think, how you use tools, and how you communicate technical problems.

---

## Task

Record a **5-minute screen recording** (with your voice narrating) where you:

1. **Run the app** from `exercise_3_room_screen_mini.dart` (or the main app via `flutter run`)
2. **Open Flutter DevTools** (Performance overlay, Widget Inspector, or Timeline)
3. **Identify at least 2 issues** visible at runtime (jank, unnecessary rebuilds, layout problems)
4. **Walk through your debugging process aloud** — explain what you're looking at, what you suspect, and why
5. **Fix at least 1 issue** and show the before/after difference

---

## Requirements

- **Must include your voice** explaining your thought process
- **Must show DevTools** or Performance overlay at some point
- **Must show the running app** (emulator or device)
- Keep it under **7 minutes** — we respect your time
- Quality of explanation matters more than number of bugs found

---

## How to Submit

1. Record using **Loom** (free), **OBS**, or your phone pointed at the screen
2. Upload to one of:
   - YouTube (unlisted)
   - Google Drive (share link with "anyone with link can view")
   - Loom (share link)
3. Put the video link in your `answers/questionnaire_answers.json` file (generated by the questionnaire)
4. Also add the link to your `README.md`

---

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Points | What We Look For |
|-----------|--------|------------------|
| DevTools usage | 5 | Actually uses Performance overlay, Widget Inspector, or Timeline — not just print statements |
| Problem identification | 5 | Correctly identifies real issues (not invented problems) |
| Debugging process | 5 | Logical approach — hypothesis, investigation, conclusion |
| Communication | 5 | Clear explanation, would be easy to follow in a team code review |

---

## Tips

- Don't rehearse a perfect script — we want to see your real thought process
- It's OK to say "I'm not sure, let me check..." — that's authentic
- If you find something unexpected, talk through it
- Show your IDE, terminal, and DevTools — not just the emulator
